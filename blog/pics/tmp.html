**Precomputed Radiance Transfer** ![Figure [PRT]: ](prt-head.png) Precomputed Radiance Transfer (PRT) is a method to precompute radiance transfer (complex light interaction) within a scene. PRT also offers you a runtime technique to evaluate the amount of radiance given the emitter information and the compressed precomputed radiance transfer. In a layman's (game developer's) term, it is a lightmap that you can also change the lighting condition! Relighting Intuition ==================== Now, we want to relight a simple scene. For example, that simple scene can be an envmap with bunny inside. Instead of using complicated techniques, I first render this scene under multiple lighting conditions. ![Figure [PRT]: Pictures of bunnies under different lighting conditions](prt-bunnies.png) Let's say, I want to see bunny in the afternoon. Sadly, I did not render an afternoon bunny rendered. However, what I can do is that I can find images with the most similar lighting conditions. Then, compute a new lighting condition through linear combination of them. In my case, I chose two images, noon and evening. The afternoon bunny image can be computed through a simple interpolation process. ![Figure [PRT]: A missing picture can be obtained through interpolation](prt-bunnies-math.png) Surely, since we have only 3 different lighting conditions. The interpolation result might look ugly. One way to counter this problem is to prerender a lot more images with different lighting conditions.
